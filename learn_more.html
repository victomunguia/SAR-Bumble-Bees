Background

In the event of a natural disaster, the greatest challenge first responders and rescuers face is to locate survivors that may trapped under rubble and debris. They have to act fast because time is of the essence. However, with large enough area it may take weeks to dig through all the accumulated debris searching for a sign of life. This is where SAR Bumble Bees come into play; powerful drones equipped with a high-quality sensor package capable of scanning an area using infrared imaging, cellphone generated signal detection, and a sensitive microphone capable of picking up the faintest noises that may come from possible survivors.


Objective

Our goal is to design a sensor package that is equipped with a multitude of sensors and instruments that are capable of working within the specified range and specifications as detailed in Table.1. Consideration has been given to the fact that these packages may be operated from an aerial position, and will need to be have a long range of detection to accommodate the varying natural disaster scenarios.

When a natural disaster like an earthquake occurs, it may span chaos over a large city, requiring multiple drones to speed up detection and enable first responders to act quickly. With that in mind, our goal is to minimize cost; each functional drone should be within the price limit of a thousand dollars while still allowing accurate detection. We have made this possible by researching economical solutions to help minimize the price of each drone.

After the disaster, many drones will be released at once to search the area. Each drone will then search a small area before moving and pausing to search again. Once any of the droneâ€™s sensors detect signs of a person, that information and the current GPS coordinates of the drone are then transmitted to a control center via a mesh network, which uses all nearby drones as a communication node to relay data without the need for existing infrastructure such as an access point. This control center relays all available information to the first responders, who can see the locations of potential victims in order to narrow down their search.


Hardware


Xbee Pro 60mW Wire Antenna

Capable of operating at a 2.4GHz, this module is a reliable and simple to use module that establishes a connection between the drones and the control center. It has a range of 1 mile, a built-in antenna, and a 250 kbps maximum data rate.


Comica CVM-V30 lite Super Cardioid Shotgun Microphone

This super microphone is capable of long time recording and noise reduction, operating anywhere from 40Hz-20 kHz. Its super cardioid pattern makes it ideal to pick up noise from one direction at a sensitivity of -34dB.


Maxbotic Inc. Ultrasonic Detection Sensor

A low power consuming module, with an easy to use interface is a high quality ultrasonic sensor that detects large objects anywhere from 6 to 240 inches, and is able to detect human presence from four feet away.


FLiR Dev Kit for Thermal Detection

With a 80 x 60 resolution camera, this thermal detector is able to detect thermal radiation with a refresh rate less than 0.5s. It is has a 51 degree focus range and takes in less than 150mW to operate.


RTL-SDR Radio Scanner

This scanner can be tuned to detect frequencies from the range of 500kHz-1.7GHz, with a 3.2MHz transmission bandwidth. It is able to detect any signal generator by personal held devices that a victim may carry on them.
